Sigiriya is one of Sri Lanka's most important historical sites. Locals refer to this ancient palace and fortress complex as the "Eighth Wonder of the World", and it attracts thousands of tourists each year for its significant archaeological attraction.

The frescoes portray naked women and are thought to be either portraits of Kasyapa's wives and concubines or religious rituals performed by priestesses. Despite the fact that the females depicted in the frescoes are unknown, these ancient artworks celebrate feminine beauty and are historically significant.

The Mirror Wall is one of Sigiriya's most stunning features. It was once polished to the point where the king could see his reflection in it. Inscriptions and poems written by Sigiriya visitors are painted on the Mirror wall.

The oldest inscriptions date back to the eighth century. Sigiriya was a tourist destination more than a thousand years ago, according to these inscriptions. Painting on the wall is now strictly forbidden.
